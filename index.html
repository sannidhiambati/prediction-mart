How Prediction Markets Work.

From Question to Payout

A prediction market is a marketplace where you can trade contracts based on the outcome of future events. The core idea is simple: the market price of a contract reflects the crowd's collective belief about the probability of an event occurring.

Each contract is tied to a simple Yes/No question. If you believe the event will happen, you buy "Yes" shares. If you think it won't, you buy "No" shares. When the event resolves, correct shares pay out at $1, and incorrect shares become worthless.

Example Transactions.

Trade 1: Economic Data

Market: "Will US quarterly GDP growth be above 2.5%?"

Your Analysis: You believe strong consumer spending will push growth higher, so you think the market's probability is too low.

  • "Yes" shares are trading at $0.60 (a 60% probability).
  • You buy 100 "Yes" shares for a total cost of $60.00.

Outcome: The official report is released, and GDP growth is 2.8%. The market resolves to "Yes".

Result: Your 100 shares are now worth $1.00 each, totaling $100.00.
Your Profit: $100 (payout) - $60 (cost) = $40.00.

Trade 2: Technological Milestone

Market: "Will SpaceX land Starship on Mars by 2030?"

Your Analysis: You think the technical challenges and timelines are vastly underestimated by the public.

  • "Yes" shares are trading at $0.22 (a 22% probability).
  • This means "No" shares are trading at $0.78 ($1.00 - $0.22).
  • You buy 50 "No" shares for a total cost of $39.00.

Outcome: The year 2030 passes, and the landing has not occurred. The market resolves to "No".

Result: Your 50 "No" shares are now worth $1.00 each, totaling $50.00.
Your Profit: $50 (payout) - $39 (cost) = $11.00.
